## Deliverable 1 - Problem Statement And Success Metrics

### Problem Statement

The legal team reviews ~300 vendor contracts per quarter, each taking ~90 minutes, resulting in ~450 hours of legal review time per quarter. Current turnaround time is 4–6 business days, creating procurement delays.

There is company playbook that covers liability caps, data processing addenda, termination clauses, IP ownership, SLA commitments, governing law, indemnity scope.

- 70% of the volume, about 210 contracts per quarter, usually matches the playbook and is therefore repetitive review work
- 20%, about 60 contracts per quarter, contains deviations that can be redlined within an established playbook
- 10%, about 30 contracts per quarter, requires senior-lawyer escalation before any counteroffer can be sent

Senior legal judgment is being consumed by first-pass clause spotting and playbook comparison instead of the 30 or so quarterly contracts that actually need lawyer attention.

Two asks:
- The current 4-6 business day turnaround is too long for the procurement team and needs to be shortened. 
- No counteroffer may leave legal's queue without a named lawyer's sign-off on the specific clauses being negotiated.

A first agentic solution should therefore reduce first-pass review effort on the 270 contracts per quarter that are standard or playbook-negotiable, while preserving strict human control over negotiated outbound positions.

### Success Metrics

1. Reduce average human review effort from 90 minutes per contract to 35 minutes or less across the full quarterly volume.
2. Reduce quarterly human review load from about 450 hours to 175 hours or less, saving about 275 hours per quarter.
3. Route at least 95% of the 210 standard contracts into a same-day "playbook match" queue with no manual clause hunting.
4. Route at least 90% of the 60 playbook-negotiable contracts into a paralegal redline queue with clause-level rationale and proposed fallback language attached.
5. Identify and escalate 100% of contracts containing must-escalate clause conditions in validation, because missing even one would break the legal risk model.
6. Cut end-to-end turnaround from 4-6 business days to 1 business day for standard contracts, 2 business days for playbook-negotiable contracts, and same-day triage for senior-lawyer escalations.
7. Enforce the governance rule that 0 counteroffers leave the legal queue without a recorded named lawyer sign-off on each negotiated clause.
