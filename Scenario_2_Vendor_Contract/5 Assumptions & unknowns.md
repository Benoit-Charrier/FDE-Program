
## Deliverable 5 - Assumptions And Unknowns

1. Assumption: the negotiation playbook can be converted into a structured format with explicit rules, approved fallback language, and clear escalation thresholds.
Unknown to validate: if the playbook only exists as informal lawyer know-how or scattered documents, the agent will not be reliable enough to build.

2. Assumption: most inbound contracts arrive as searchable PDFs or DOCX files.
Unknown to validate: what percentage of the real intake volume is scanned, image-based, or badly formatted enough to break clause extraction.

3. Assumption: the paralegal truly has authority to apply a defined subset of redlines without re-reviewing every clause from scratch.
Unknown to validate: how that authority is currently documented and where the actual boundary sits in practice.

4. Assumption: named lawyer sign-off can be captured electronically at the clause level.
Unknown to validate: whether the client has an approval system that can store clause-specific approvals, timestamps, and approver identity in an auditable way.

5. Assumption: the seven clause families listed in the scenario represent the main review burden.
Unknown to validate: whether other clauses, such as security, audit rights, limitation carve-outs, or insurance, materially affect routing frequency.

6. Assumption: historical contracts and outcomes are available for testing and calibration.
Unknown to validate: how many prior contracts exist, whether their final dispositions are labeled, and whether privileged content can be used for model evaluation.

7. Assumption: procurement and legal are aligned on what "faster turnaround" should mean operationally.
Unknown to validate: whether the target should be same-day triage, 24-hour response, or some other SLA by contract type.

8. Assumption: the organization is comfortable with the agent preparing redlines as long as a human remains in control of release.
Unknown to validate: whether there are policy or client-commitment constraints that require a lawyer to draft all negotiation language personally.

9. Assumption: the contract intake and document-routing systems can be integrated without major process redesign.
Unknown to validate: whether the current workflow is email-based, CLM-based, or split across tools in a way that makes automation harder than the legal reasoning itself.
